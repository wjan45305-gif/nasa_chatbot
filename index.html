Pioneering the Planet Explorers

Voyager 1 and Voyager 2 are NASA‚Äôs most iconic space explorers, launched in 1977 to study the outer planets and now journeying through interstellar space. Voyager 1 is the farthest human-made object from Earth, having crossed into interstellar space in 2012, while Voyager 2 remains the only spacecraft to have visited all four gas giants Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Together, they revealed stunning details about planetary rings, moons, magnetic fields, and distant atmospheres, transforming our understanding of the solar system. Both spacecraft carry the famous Golden Record, a time capsule with sounds and greetings from Earth, sent as a message to any intelligent life that may one day encounter them. Even after four decades, the Voyagers continue to transmit invaluable data back to Earth, embodying humanity‚Äôs first great leap into the vast cosmic ocean.

MISSION OPERATIONS

Kepler-442b Investigation

Comprehensive atmospheric analysis of this super-Earth located 1,206 light-years away. Studying potential biosignatures and atmospheric composition using advanced spectroscopy techniques.

Active

Proxima Centauri Survey

Long-term monitoring mission of our nearest stellar neighbor, focusing on Proxima b's habitability potential and atmospheric dynamics during stellar flare events.

Ongoing

Europa Deep Dive

Robotic exploration beneath Europa's ice sheet, searching for microbial life in the subsurface ocean and analyzing water samples from geysers.

Planning

Atmospheric Modeling

Developing advanced computer models to predict exoplanet atmospheric conditions and identify optimal candidates for follow-up observations with next-generation telescopes.

Active

Biosignature Database

Creating comprehensive catalog of potential biological markers that could indicate life on distant worlds, including both Earth-like and exotic biochemistries.

Research

Interstellar Communication

Designing protocols for potential communication with extraterrestrial intelligence, including mathematical languages and universal concepts.

Development

TOI-715b Confirmation

Recently confirmed this Earth-sized exoplanet in the habitable zone of a nearby red dwarf star, making it a prime candidate for atmospheric studies.

Confirmed

Water Vapor Detection

First detection of water vapor in the atmosphere of K2-18b, a sub-Neptune exoplanet in its star's habitable zone, using Hubble Space Telescope data.

Published

Multi-Planet System

Discovery of a seven-planet system around TRAPPIST-1, with three planets potentially habitable and showing signs of atmospheric retention.

Complete

James Webb Follow-up

Planned observations using JWST to study atmospheric composition of newly discovered potentially habitable exoplanets, focusing on oxygen and methane detection.

Scheduled

Breakthrough Starshot

Collaboration on developing ultra-light nanocrafts capable of reaching Alpha Centauri within 20 years to capture close-up images of Proxima b.

Planning

Mars Sample Return

Leading the astrobiology analysis team for the Mars Sample Return mission, searching for ancient microbial life in carefully selected rock samples.
